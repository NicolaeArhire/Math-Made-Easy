The very beginning of mathematics:

Info: People kept notes of the lunar calendar making notches on a baboon fibula bone

Initiator(s): Ishango tribe

When: ~20000 B.C.

Where: Zaire

Nowadays: Such notched bones still exist in several museums

Fun Fact: Numbers greater than 2 didn't existed yet; the numerical system was "one", "two" and "many"

Sumerian mathematics:

Info: Sumeria, a.k.a. Cradle of Civilization, begun analyzing symbols & large numbers

Initiator(s): Mesopotamian people

When: ~6000 B.C.

Where: Mesopotamia (now Iraq)

Nowadays: Some of their tablets are still researched by archeologists

Fun Fact: Apparently, the mesopotamians used tablets before the electricity was invented

Egyptian mathematics:

Info: A decimal system was created based on body measurements as units

Initiator(s): Egyptian people

When: ~2000 B.C.

Where: Egypt

Nowadays: Binary numbers are the foundation for almost anything

Fun Fact: We are still wondering why egyptians represented numbers as people dancing & drinking

Greek mathematics:

Info: Big step ahead in developing theorems and geometric formulas

Initiator(s): Pythagora, Thales, Zeno

When: ~400 B.C.

Where: Greece

Nowadays: Most of the technology fields have greek math roots

Fun Fact: No fun fact. We would've had real-life Flintstones without these guys

Hellenistic mathematics:

Info: Algorithms for identifying prime numbers were invented

Initiator(s): Euclid, Archimedes, Heron, Diophantus

When: ~100 B.C.

Where: Alexandria (Egypt)

Nowadays: This period had the first female mathematician (Hypatia)

Fun Fact: That was the moment when male mathematicians began to feel a little competition

"Renaissance" movement:

Info: Mathematics as we know it was standardized (multiplication, radical, root, equations)

Initiator(s): Newton, Leibniz, Copernic, Galilei, Tartaglia

When: ~1300-1700 A.D.

Where: Italy, Germany

Nowadays: First algebra book still exist

Fun Fact: So they are responsible for those headaches....

Modern mathematics:

Info: Machine learning and artificial intelligence at it's peak

Initiator(s): Vladimir Vapnik, Andrew Ng, Cassie Kozyrov, Yann LeCun

When: As you read this

Where: Worlwide

Nowadays: Internet speeds up the progress

Fun Fact: Everyone can be a mathematician👌

Euler-Legrange ecuation

The Euler-Lagrange differential equation is the fundamental equation of calculus of variations.

Cauchy-Riemann equations

This pair of equations imply that the components of a complex analytical function are always the solutions of the Laplacian equation.

Gaussian divergence theorem

This theorem has applications almost everywhere (applied mathematics, engineering, physics).

Cayley-Hamilton theorem

This theorem helps solving matrix equations using the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the matrices.

Fundamental theorem of Algebra

This theorem is a fundamental result for polynomial equations regarding their solutions.
